The Life of Spider-man

His real name is Peter Parker, a young man from New York City who gains spider-like abilities—such as wall-crawling, enhanced strength, agility, and a "spider-sense"—after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

Early Life and Career

Before becoming a superhero, the early life of Spider-Man's alter ego, Peter Parker, was defined by his humble upbringing as a bullied orphan and brilliant student in Queens. His life took a dramatic turn when a radioactive spider bite gave him superpowers, forcing him to confront the responsibility that comes with his new abilities.

Spider-man's First Battle

The first major villain that Spider-man battled in the live action films was the Green Goblin (Norman Osborn), after gaining superhuman abilities and becoming mentally unstable. Spider-Man’s final battle with the Green Goblin happens in an abandoned building, where they fight brutally. Green Goblin injures Spider-Man badly, but when he tries to kill him with his glider, Spider-Man dodges, and the glider impales the Green Goblin instead, killing him.
